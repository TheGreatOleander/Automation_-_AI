12.3 Evaluation-Driven Development

The single highest-leverage practice in AI automation development is building your evaluation suite before building the automation. An eval suite is the instrument that tells you whether a change made things better or worse. Without it, every change is a guess.

The Eval Suite Structure

  • Unit evals: 30-100 individual test cases with known-correct outputs. Test the specific tasks your automation performs.
  • Integration evals: 5-15 end-to-end workflow runs. Test that the components connect correctly and produce the expected final output.
  • Regression evals: The subset of unit evals that previously failed. Re-run after every fix to confirm the fix holds.
  • Adversarial evals: Deliberately malformed, empty, very long, and injection-attempt inputs. Confirm the system handles them gracefully.

12.4 LLM-as-Judge for Open-Ended Evals

For tasks where the correct output cannot be expressed as an exact string (summaries, analyses, rewrites), use a separate AI call to evaluate the output. This is the 'LLM-as-judge' pattern. Use it carefully: the judge model has its own biases.
